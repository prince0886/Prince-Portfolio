About Me

I'm passionate about blending technology anddevelopment to create intuitive solutions thatconnect with people.

Wait, I'm an Engineer?

I'm a third-year CSE student at IIITG (Class of 2026—yes, I still survive on caffeine and console.logs). I figured building responsive websites is more fun than responding to hardware errors. Now I spend my days turning divs into dreams and bugs into features. You're welcome, internet.

Here's my story!

How I Discovered Development

Let’s just say I stumbled into web development after years of yelling at websites that broke if you so much as blinked wrong. One day, it hit me maybe someone should care if the internet actually works for people (radical idea, I know). So, I ditched my old design job where “can you make it more web 3.0?” was considered solid feedback for the chaotic beauty of code. Broken layouts? Plenty. Sleepless nights chasing a rogue semicolon? More than I’d like to admit. Competence? Lightly toasted. But hey, at least I’m not deploying sites that crash harder than Internet Explorer on a good day.

DSA Was My Toxic Ex

I've been doing DSA for 3–4 years, which basically means I’ve spent thousands of hours wrestling with code and silently judging websites that take 12 business days to load. By May 2024, I finally snapped. Enter: Web Development. Turns out, building things people actually enjoy using (and that don’t make them want to yeet their laptops into a black hole) is way more fun. Who knew?
